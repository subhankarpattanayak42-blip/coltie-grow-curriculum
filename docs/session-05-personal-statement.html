Session 5: Personal Statement — Your Story

Duration: 90 minutes | Format: Live | Instructors: Subhankar & Anuj

Session Objectives

By the end of this session, students will be able to: 1. Identify the key elements of a compelling personal statement 2. Brainstorm 3 potential essay topics using the “identity moments” method 3. Write a draft opening paragraph for their personal statement 4. Use AI for feedback without losing their voice

Session Flow

Time Segment Activity Lead

0:10–0:25 What Makes a Great Essay Anatomy of a personal statement — examples from real admits Subhankar

Content Notes

Anatomy of a Personal Statement

The Common App essay (US): 650 words. Prompt of your choice. The UCAS Personal Statement (UK): 4,000 characters. One essay covering all choices.

What admissions officers look for: 1. Voice — Does this sound like a real teenager or a generic template? 2. Insight — What did you learn about yourself or the world? 3. Specificity — Concrete details beat vague generalities every time 4. Growth — How did you change? What did you overcome? 5. Curiosity — Are you interesting to have in a classroom?

What to avoid: - Listing achievements (that’s what the activities section is for) - Writing what you think they want to hear - The “I want to change the world” cliché without personal connection - AI-generated content (most admissions officers can spot it)

Identity Moments Exercise

Prompt: “Think about 5 moments in your life that changed how you see yourself or the world.”

Moment Type Examples
Challenge A failure, disappointment, or difficult situation
Discovery When you found something you love
Connection A relationship that shaped you
Perspective Shift When you realized something important
Choice A decision you made that defined you

AI Feedback Prompt (for AFTER they write):

“I’m a high school student writing my college application essay. Here is my draft. Don’t rewrite it. Instead: 1. Tell me what you think my main message is 2. Point out any parts that feel generic 3. Ask me 3 questions that could help me go deeper 4. Suggest one sentence I should cut”

Activities

  1. Before/After Rewrite: Show a bad personal statement opening. Students rewrite it.
  2. Identity Mapping: Visual exercise — students draw their life journey as a timeline, mark the 5 moments
  3. Silent Writing Sprint: 10 minutes of focused writing with no editing

Homework

  1. First Draft (1 hour): Write a complete first draft of your personal statement.
  2. US applicants: 650 words for Common App
  3. UK applicants: 4,000 characters for UCAS

  4. Other countries: follow your system’s requirements

  5. Peer Review Prep: Bring your draft to next session for peer + AI feedback

  6. Read: One sample essay from the provided collection. Note: what makes it work?
