COSTAR Prompt Engineering Framework

A structured approach to writing effective AI prompts.

Letter Stands For Ask Yourself
C Context What’s the background? Who am I? What’s the situation?
O Objective What exactly do I want the AI to do?
S Style What format or writing style? (Essay, list, email, dialogue)
T Tone What tone? (Formal, casual, persuasive, humorous)
A Audience Who is this for? (Teacher, admissions officer, friend)
R Response What should the output look like? (Length, format, structure)

Bad Prompt → Good Prompt Examples

Example 1: Career Research

Bad: “Tell me about computer science careers”

Good: “I’m a 10th grader interested in both technology and helping people (my Holland Code is SIA). Suggest 5 careers that combine CS with social impact. For each: job title, what they do, salary range in India, and which college majors lead to it.”

Example 2: Essay Feedback

Bad: “Is my essay good?”

Good: “I’m a high school student applying to US universities. Here’s my personal statement draft. Don’t rewrite it. Instead: 1) Tell me what you think my main message is 2) Point out parts that feel generic 3) Ask 3 questions to help me go deeper.”

Example 3: Study Help

Bad: “Explain photosynthesis”

Good: “I’m in Grade 10 CBSE biology. I understand the basics of photosynthesis but get confused about the light-dependent vs light-independent reactions. Explain the difference using an analogy I can remember. Keep it under 200 words.”
