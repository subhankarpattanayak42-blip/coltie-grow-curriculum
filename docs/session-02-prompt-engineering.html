Session 2: Prompt Engineering & AI Ethics

Duration: 90 minutes | Format: Live | Instructors: Subhankar & Anuj

Apply an ethics checklist before using AI for academic work 4. Use AI as a brainstorming partner, not a replacement

Content Notes

How LLMs Work (Analogy)

“Imagine you’ve read every book, article, and Reddit thread ever written. Someone says the first half of a sentence, and your job is to guess the most likely next word — over and over. That’s what an LLM does. It’s not thinking. It’s predicting.”

Key concepts: - Tokens = words or sub-words the model processes - Training data = everything the model has read - Temperature = how creative (risky) the output is - Context window = how much the model “remembers” in one conversation

COSTAR Prompt Framework

Letter Stands For Question to Ask
C Context What’s the background? Who am I?
O Objective What do I want the AI to do?
S Style What tone/format should it use?
T Tone Formal, casual, persuasive, neutral?
A Audience Who is this for?
R Response What format should the output take?

Live Demo — Bad vs Good Prompt: - Bad: “Write about climate change” - Good: “I’m a 10th grader writing a persuasive essay for my English class. The audience is my teacher. Write a 300-word opening paragraph arguing that individual action on climate change matters. Use a confident but not aggressive tone. Include one statistic from a credible source.”

AI Ethics Checklist (For Students)

Before using AI for any assignment, ask: 1. ✅ Would my teacher approve? If you’d hide it, don’t do it. 2. ✅ Did I do the thinking first? AI should refine your ideas, not create them from zero. 3. ✅ Did I verify the facts? AI makes things up (hallucinates). 4. ✅ Is this my voice? The final output should sound like YOU. 5. ✅ Did I disclose? When in doubt, cite AI as a tool.

Activities

  1. Prompt Fix Challenge: Show 3 bad prompts → students rewrite using COSTAR
  2. Spot the Hallucination: Show AI output with one fake fact — students find it
  3. Pair Prompt-Off: Students pair up, each writes a prompt, evaluates partner’s result

Homework

  1. Prompt Portfolio (45 min): Write 3 prompts using COSTAR framework for:
  2. Brainstorming college essay topics
  3. Explaining a concept you’re struggling with in school

  4. Planning a weekly study schedule

  5. Read: AI Hallucinations: Why ChatGPT Makes Things Up (5-min read)

  6. Reflection: Write 2-3 sentences on your personal AI ethics policy. What will you use AI for? What won’t you?
