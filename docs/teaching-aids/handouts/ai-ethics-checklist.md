# AI Ethics Checklist — For Students

**Before you use AI for any assignment, ask yourself these 5 questions:**

---

## 1. Would my teacher approve? 
If you'd hide it, don't do it.

## 2. Did I do the thinking first?
AI should refine YOUR ideas, not create them from zero. Always write/draw/think before prompting.

## 3. Did I verify the facts?
AI makes things up (hallucinations). Check every statistic, date, and claim against a real source.

## 4. Is this my voice?
After using AI, rewrite the output in your own words. The final product should sound like YOU.

## 5. Did I disclose?
When in doubt, cite AI as a tool. Most schools now have AI use policies — follow them.

---

## The Green Light / Red Light Test

| Use Case | 🟢 Allowed | 🔴 Not Allowed |
|----------|-----------|----------------|
| Brainstorming essay topics | ✅ | — |
| Asking for feedback on YOUR draft | ✅ | — |
| Generating a full essay from scratch | — | ✅ |
| Explaining a concept you don't understand | ✅ | — |
| Writing a teacher email for you | — | ✅ |
| Checking grammar and spelling | ✅ | — |
| Summarizing a long article | ✅ (read original too) | — |
| Creating images for your presentation | ✅ | — |
| Solving math problems for you | — | ✅ |
| Translating a foreign language text | ✅ | — |

---

## How to Disclose AI Use

**In your essay/appendix:**
> *"I used ChatGPT to brainstorm topic ideas and get feedback on my draft. All final content and ideas are my own."*

**To your teacher:**
> *"I used [tool name] to [specific use]. I'd be happy to show you my process."*

---

## Red Flags: AI Overuse Warning Signs

- 🔴 You can't write a sentence without AI
- 🔴 The output doesn't sound like you
- 🔴 You don't understand what the AI wrote
- 🔴 You feel dishonest about your process
- 🔴 You skipped the thinking step entirely

**Remember: AI is a thinking partner, not a shortcut.**
