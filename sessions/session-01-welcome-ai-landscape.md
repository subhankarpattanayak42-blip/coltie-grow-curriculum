## Content Notes

### What is AI? (Teaching Script)

**Hook:** "Raise your hand if you've used ChatGPT. Now raise your hand if you've asked it to write an essay for you. (Pause.) OK — raise your hand if you've felt guilty about it."

**Points to cover:**
- AI is not magic — it's pattern recognition at scale
- The three types (use simple cars analogy):
  - **Narrow AI** = a GPS that only knows one route (most AI today)
  - **General AI** = a human driver who can handle any road (doesn't exist yet)
  - **Superintelligence** = a driver who's better than every human (theoretical)
- Everyday AI: recommendation engines (Netflix, YouTube), navigation (Google Maps), facial recognition, voice assistants, spam filters
- LLMs (Large Language Models): "A computer that has read most of the internet and can predict the next word — really, really well"

### AI in the Wild Activity

Break students into breakout rooms of 4-5. Each group gets 10 minutes to list:
1. 3 apps they used today that use AI
2. What they think the AI is doing
3. One creepy thing AI does

Reconvene and share highlights. Use this to transition to the ethics conversation (Session 2 preview).

## Common Student Questions & Answers

**Q: "Is AI going to take my job?"**
A: "AI won't take your job. A person who knows how to use AI will. That's why you're here."

**Q: "Do I need to learn to code to understand AI?"**
A: "No. You need to learn to think. Coding helps, but understanding concepts matters more. We'll use no-code tools in this program."

**Q: "Is ChatGPT cheating?"**
A: "Using ChatGPT to do your work for you is cheating. Using it as a thinking partner — to brainstorm, refine, critique — is a skill colleges want."
