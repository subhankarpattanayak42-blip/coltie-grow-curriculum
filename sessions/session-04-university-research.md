# Session 4: University Research & Shortlisting

**Duration:** 90 minutes | **Format:** Live | **Instructors:** Subhankar & Anuj

---

## Session Objectives

By the end of this session, students will be able to:
1. Apply an 8-factor rubric to evaluate universities
2. Search and shortlist universities matching their priorities
3. Understand the difference between Reach, Match, and Safety schools
4. Create a personalized university shortlist of 8-10 schools

## Content Notes

### Global University Landscape

| Country | Strengths | Application System | Cost Range (Annual) |
|---------|-----------|-------------------|---------------------|
| **USA** | Liberal arts, flexibility, research, diverse options | Common App, Coalition App | $20K–$80K |
| **UK** | Focused degrees (3 years), prestige | UCAS | £15K–£40K |
| **Canada** | Affordable, welcoming, strong STEM | OUAC + provincial systems | CAD $15K–$50K |
| **India** | Low cost, Central/State universities, IITs/IISc | CUET, JEE, NEET, individual apps | ₹20K–₹3L |
| **Singapore** | World-class STEM, Asian hub | NUS/NTU individual apps | SGD $20K–$40K |
| **Australia** | Strong research, pathway programs | UAC, direct apps | AUD $25K–$50K |
| **Europe** | Low/no tuition (Germany, Norway, France), English programs | Varies by country | €0–€20K |

### 8-Factor University Rubric

| Factor | What to Evaluate | Weight (Your Choice) |
|--------|-----------------|---------------------|
| **1. Academic Fit** | Programs offered, curriculum structure, faculty expertise | ★★★★★ |
| **2. Career Outcomes** | Placement rates, internship opportunities, alumni network | ★★★★★ |
| **3. Location & Culture** | City vs campus, climate, diversity, student life | ★★★★ |
| **4. Cost & Scholarships** | Tuition, living costs, merit aid, need-based aid | ★★★★★ |
| **5. Selectivity** | Acceptance rate, average GPA/test scores of admitted students | ★★★★ |
| **6. Size & Resources** | Student-faculty ratio, lab facilities, library, research centers | ★★★ |
| **7. International Support** | Visa assistance, orientation, international student office | ★★★ |
| **8. Reputation & Rankings** | Global brand value, specific program rankings | ★★★ |

### Reach-Match-Safety Model

| Category | Definition | Examples in Shortlist |
|----------|-----------|---------------------|
| **Reach (2-3)** | Your profile is below their average admit. 15-30% acceptance rate range. | Stanford, MIT, Oxford |
| **Match (3-4)** | Your profile aligns with their average admit. 40-60% range. | UT Austin, University of Toronto |
| **Safety (3-4)** | You exceed their average profile. 70%+ acceptance rate. | Arizona State, University of San Francisco |

### AI Prompts for University Research

> "I'm a Grade 11 student from India interested in Computer Science. Suggest 10 universities in the US that are strong in AI/ML research, with acceptance rates between 20-50%, and offer merit scholarships to international students."

> "Compare University of Toronto, University of British Columbia, and University of Waterloo for a Computer Science undergraduate degree. Compare: tuition for international students, co-op programs, AI research strength, and location quality of life."

---

## Activities

1. **Rubric Building:** Students assign their own weights to the 8 factors
2. **Live University Search:** Using web + AI to research 3 universities in real time
3. **Shortlist Drafting:** Fill in the Reach-Match-Safety template

---

## Homework

1. **University Shortlist (1 hour):** Complete your 8-10 school shortlist using the rubric. List:
   - School name, country
   - Why it's on your list (which factors)
   - Reach/Match/Safety classification
   - One question you need answered about each

2. **Research Deep Dive:** Pick ONE school from your shortlist. Find:
   - The application deadline
   - Application fee
   - Essay prompts (if US) or personal statement requirements (if UK)
   - SAT/ACT or standardized test policy

3. **Upload to COLTIE:** Save your shortlist in your COLTIE profile
